Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about GLF

How are donations used?

Your donations directly fund our programs in education, disaster relief, healthcare, and community development. We ensure that at least 85% of all donations go directly to program implementation, with only 10% for fundraising and 5% for administration costs.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes, Gamers Lounge Foundation is a registered charitable organization. All donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. You'll receive a receipt for your records that can be used for tax purposes.

How can I volunteer with GLF?

We welcome volunteers! You can fill out the contact form above and select "Volunteer Opportunity" as the subject. We'll get back to you with available opportunities in your area. We need volunteers for event organization, fundraising, teaching, medical support, and more.

Can I donate items instead of money?

Yes! We accept donations of school supplies, books, clothing, non-perishable food items, and medical supplies. Please contact us to arrange a drop-off or to discuss other donation options.

How do I start a fundraising campaign for GLF?

We'd love to help you fundraise! Contact us with your idea and we'll provide you with materials, guidance, and support. You can organize events, birthday fundraisers, gaming tournaments, or any creative idea you have.

How can my company/organization partner with GLF?

We welcome corporate partnerships! Please select "Partnership" as the subject in the contact form, and our team will reach out to discuss sponsorship opportunities, matching gifts, employee engagement programs, and more.

How do I report an issue or provide feedback?

We take feedback seriously. Please use the contact form to report any issues or share your suggestions. You can also reach out directly to our leadership team. We're committed to continuous improvement.

What areas does GLF work in?

While based in Ghana, West Africa, we work with partners across multiple continents. Our main focus areas include Sub-Saharan Africa, but we also support disaster relief efforts globally when needed.
